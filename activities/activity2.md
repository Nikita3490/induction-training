# Activity 2: What is Education in India?

Education in India is a large, diverse system overseen by both central and state governments.

## Structure
- Pre-primary (playschool/kindergarten)
- Primary (Grades 1–5)
- Upper primary/middle (Grades 6–8)
- Secondary (Grades 9–10)
- Higher secondary (Grades 11–12)
- Higher education (universities, colleges, technical and professional institutes)

## Governance
- The Ministry of Education sets national policy.
- Central bodies like NCERT, CBSE, UGC, and AICTE manage curriculum, exams, and institution standards.
- States run most public schools and administer many exams.

## Key Features
- The Right to Education Act guarantees free, compulsory schooling for ages 6–14.
- The National Education Policy 2020 emphasizes foundational literacy, vocational learning, multidisciplinary higher education, and digital access.
- India has a mix of public, private, and religious schools, plus technical institutes like IITs and IIMs.

## Common Challenges
- Uneven quality across regions.
- Access gaps in rural areas.
- High-stakes exams and rote learning pressure.
- The need for better teacher training and infrastructure.

## Summary
Indian education is a multi-tiered national system with growing reform efforts, but it still faces equity and quality issues.
